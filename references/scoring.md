# Scoring Rubric: 0-100 per entry

**100 points = a full day's worth of human output.** Not "an impressive day," just "a normal complete day for a human with a body, a brain, and feelings." If the user logs a day and totals over 100, they crushed it. If they log a day and total under 50, they had a hard day and the entries still count.

## How to score

Start with the **raw effort tier**. Then apply **multipliers** if context warrants.

### Raw effort tiers

| Range | What it looks like | Examples |
|-------|---------------------|----------|
| **1-10** | Tiny but real. Friction was the win. | Got out of bed, drank water, replied "k", brushed teeth on a regular day |
| **11-25** | Normal small thing. One unit of effort. | One email, one bill paid, one chore, ate a real meal, a 20-min walk, a quick errand |
| **26-50** | Real lift. Took focus or care. | Hard conversation, 1-2 hour focused work block, cooking a full meal from scratch, a workout, a therapy session, a doctor appointment |
| **51-75** | Heavy. Drained your battery. | A long drained meeting, a conflict navigated, a big creative push, a hard call you'd been avoiding, a half-day of focused work, family event you had to mask through |
| **76-100** | Huge. The thing that defines the day. | Funeral, surgery, day-long crisis, big project finished, moving house, a major decision made, a job interview, a presentation, full day of caregiving |

### Multipliers (apply when context supports it)

**Bad-day multiplier (x1.5 to x2):**
Apply if recent entries or chat context mention any of: sick, hungover, depression spike, anxiety spike, period, grief day, no sleep, post-trauma, post-crisis. Brushing teeth on a depression day is a 15, not an 8.

**Invisible labor multiplier (x1.3 to x1.5):**
Apply when the user almost didn't log something because it "doesn't count." If they include a self-deprecating phrase ("I literally just," "all I did was," "I should have done more"), that's a signal they're discounting real work. Bump the score.

**ADHD executive function tax (additive +5 to +15):**
Apply when the activity required transitioning between tasks, initiating something they'd been avoiding, or finishing something they almost didn't. Starting the dishes when you've been avoiding them all week is worth more than starting them on a good day.

**First-time-in-a-while multiplier (x1.5):**
Apply when the user mentions they "finally" did something. Finally answered that text, finally did laundry, finally made the appointment. The threshold to start is the cost, not the task itself.

### Caps and floors

- **Minimum score for any logged entry: 1.** If they logged it, it counted.
- **Maximum score for a single entry: 100.** A single thing can't be more than a full day. If they had multiple huge things in one day (funeral AND surgery AND moving), that's just a high day total, score each separately.
- **Rest entries: 5 to 30 typically.** Deliberate rest = 15-25. Doomscrolling-as-rest = 5-10. A full restorative nap or a deliberate day off = 25-30.

### When in doubt

Score generously, not stingily. The whole point of this skill is to combat the brain's tendency to discount. If you're genuinely torn between two numbers, pick the higher one.

### Examples (for calibration)

- "brushed teeth, took meds" on a normal day → **10**
- "brushed teeth, took meds" on a depression day → **18** (bad-day x1.8)
- "answered avi's text" → **8**
- "finally answered avi's text after avoiding it for 3 days" → **20** (first-time x1.5 + ADHD tax +5)
- "30 min focused work on the proposal" → **25**
- "hard call with mom" → **50** (emotional, no multiplier needed; this is baseline heavy)
- "hard call with mom while also having the flu" → **75** (bad-day x1.5)
- "watched 2 episodes of a show I actually like, no doomscrolling" → **22**
- "stared at ceiling for an hour thinking about whether to apply to funeral director school" → **35** (Big Brain Hours, real decision work)
- "went to grandma's funeral" → **90**
- "showed up to grandma's funeral while also being the one organizing food after" → **100**
