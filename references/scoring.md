# Scoring Rubric: 0-100 per entry

**100 points = a full day's worth of human output.** Not "an impressive day," just "a normal complete day for a human with a body, a brain, and feelings." If the user logs a day and totals over 100, they did A LOT. If they log a day and total under 50, they had a hard day and the entries still count.

## How to score

Start with the **raw effort tier**. Then apply **multipliers** if context warrants.

### Raw effort tiers

| Range | What it looks like | Examples |
|-------|---------------------|----------|
| **1-5** | Tiny but real. Friction was the win. | Got out of bed, drank water, replied "k", brushed teeth on a regular day, took a single pill |
| **6-15** | Normal small thing. One unit of effort. | One email, one bill paid, one quick chore, ate a real meal, a 20-min walk, a short errand |
| **16-35** | Real lift. Took focus or care. | Hard conversation, 30-60 min focused work block, cooking a full meal from scratch, a workout, a therapy session, a doctor appointment, a meeting that drained you |
| **36-65** | Heavy. Drained your battery. | Multi-hour focused work block, a hard call you'd been avoiding, navigated a conflict, big creative push, half-day of caregiving, family event you had to mask through |
| **66-100** | Huge. One thing that defined the day. | Funeral, surgery, day-long crisis, finished a major project, moving house, big decision made, job interview, presentation, full day of caregiving, hospitalization |

### What "a full day" feels like in this rubric

A normal-ish day for someone with ADHD might be:
- Brushed teeth, took meds (5 + 3 = 8)
- Real breakfast (10)
- Two focused work blocks (25 + 20 = 45)
- One bill paid (5)
- Made dinner (12)
- Watched a show, real rest (15)

Total: 100. That's a complete normal day. Crossing 100 means you went beyond that. The brain will still try to discount it. The number is there so you don't have to argue alone.

### Multipliers (apply when context supports it)

**Bad-day multiplier (x1.5 to x2):**
Apply if recent entries or chat context mention any of: sick, hungover, depression spike, anxiety spike, period, grief day, no sleep, post-trauma, post-crisis. Brushing teeth on a depression day is a 7, not a 4.

**Invisible labor multiplier (x1.3 to x1.5):**
Apply when the user almost didn't log something because it "doesn't count." If they include a self-deprecating phrase ("I literally just," "all I did was," "I should have done more"), that's a signal they're discounting real work. Bump the score.

**ADHD executive function tax (additive +1 to +5):**
Apply when the activity required transitioning between tasks, initiating something they'd been avoiding, or finishing something they almost didn't. Starting the dishes when you've been avoiding them all week is worth more than starting them on a good day. Small bump, but it adds up across the day.

**First-time-in-a-while multiplier (x1.5):**
Apply when the user mentions they "finally" did something. Finally answered that text, finally did laundry, finally made the appointment. The threshold to start is the cost, not the task itself.

### Caps and floors

- **Minimum score for any logged entry: 1.** If they logged it, it counted.
- **Maximum score for a single entry: 100.** A single thing can't be more than a full day. If they had multiple huge things in one day (funeral AND surgery), score each separately and let the day total go high.
- **Rest entries: 3 to 25 typically.** Deliberate rest = 12-20. Doomscrolling-as-rest = 3-8. A full restorative nap or a deliberate day off block = 20-25.

### When in doubt

Score generously, not stingily, but stay honest. The point isn't to inflate to 200 every day. The point is that the math reflects reality so a real big day reads as a real big day, and a real hard day reads as a real hard day, and the brain has something honest to argue with.

### Examples (for calibration)

- "Brushed teeth, took meds" on a normal day → **5**
- "Brushed teeth, took meds" on a depression day → **8** (bad-day x1.6)
- "Answered Avi's text" → **5**
- "Finally answered Avi's text after avoiding it for 3 days" → **10** (first-time x1.5 + ADHD tax +2)
- "30 min focused work on the proposal" → **20**
- "2-hour focused work block, full draft done" → **45**
- "Hard call with mom" → **40** (emotional, no multiplier needed)
- "Hard call with mom while also having the flu" → **60** (bad-day x1.5)
- "Watched 2 episodes of a show I actually like, no doomscrolling" → **15**
- "Stared at ceiling for an hour thinking about whether to apply to funeral director school" → **25** (Big Brain Hours, real decision work)
- "Built a Claude Code skill from scratch (design + 7 files + voice doc)" → **70** (Creative Time, multi-hour focused build, no multipliers)
- "Went to grandma's funeral" → **90**
- "Showed up to grandma's funeral while also being the one organizing food after" → **100**

### Day-total milestones

Use these to calibrate expectations of what totals MEAN:

| Total | Means |
|-------|-------|
| Under 30 | A hard day. Sick, depressed, grieving, surviving. The entries still count. |
| 30-60 | A small day. Some normal life happened. |
| 60-100 | A solid day. You did the things. |
| **100** | A full day. Anchor point. |
| 100-150 | Above baseline. You did A LOT. Brain will try to discount it. |
| 150-200 | Heavy day. Rest tomorrow. |
| 200+ | "WTF go lie down" tier. Real. Should be rare. |
