# The 6 Categories

Every entry gets exactly one of these. If something genuinely spans two (e.g. "had a hard conversation while on a walk"), pick the one that took the most out of the user. When unsure, ask yourself: "what did this cost them?"

---

## 1. Small Tasks

The actual to-do list shit. Errands, admin, life-maintenance, replies, transactions, chores. Things with a clear start and stop that visible-productivity culture would actually count.

**What counts:**
- Sending a text or email reply
- Paying a bill, scheduling an appointment
- Doing dishes, taking out trash, putting in laundry
- A grocery run, a pharmacy pickup
- Filling out a form, signing a thing
- "Quick" 5-minute work tasks that you actually finished

**What doesn't go here:** anything that drained you emotionally (that's Emotional Stuffs) or required deep thought (that's Big Brain Hours).

---

## 2. Self-Care

Anything that kept your body alive and functioning. The bar is on the floor. Brushing your teeth on a depression day belongs here. So does therapy, meds, a real meal, a shower, a walk, going to the doctor.

**What counts:**
- Eating an actual meal (not just snacks)
- Taking meds, taking vitamins
- Hygiene: shower, teeth, skincare, getting dressed
- Sleep stuff: going to bed at a real hour, taking a nap when needed
- Therapy appointment, doctor's appointment
- Movement: a walk, a stretch, a workout
- Drinking water (yes, if it was a deliberate act)

**What doesn't go here:** "rest" in the sense of zoning out (that's Rest). Self-care here means actively doing something for your body.

---

## 3. Emotional Stuffs

Feelings work. The thing that's invisible to everyone else and exhausts you the most. Hard conversations, holding space, processing, masking, navigating someone else's mood, grief, sitting with anxiety, conflict, repair.

**What counts:**
- A hard conversation with anyone (family, partner, friend, coworker, stranger)
- Holding space for someone else's hard day
- Crying it out, sitting with a feeling, processing
- Masking through a social event when you didn't want to be there
- Saying no to something (this is real work for ADHD brains)
- Setting a boundary, repairing after a conflict
- Grief days, anxiety days, depression check-ins with yourself
- Therapy goes in Self-Care, but processing what came up in therapy goes here

**Multiplier:** emotional labor is chronically discounted. Score generously here.

---

## 4. Big Brain Hours

Deep thinking, focused cognitive work, decisions, learning, problem-solving. Anything that left your brain feeling like a wet noodle after.

**What counts:**
- Focused work block (writing, coding, designing, analyzing)
- Reading something dense and actually retaining it
- Making a hard decision (researching, weighing options)
- Learning a new skill or studying
- Planning something complex (a trip, a project, a move)
- Long-form thinking, even if it looks like "staring at the ceiling for 20 minutes"
- Sitting with a problem until you cracked it

**Important:** "stared at ceiling thinking about funeral director school" is Big Brain Hours, not Rest. If your brain was working, it counts.

---

## 5. Creative Time

Making something. Output that didn't exist before. Doesn't matter if it's "good" or finished or shared.

**What counts:**
- Writing (anything: a tweet, a paragraph, a draft, a journal entry)
- Drawing, painting, sketching, designing
- Music: playing, writing, recording
- Cooking something new (recipe-following is Self-Care, invention is Creative)
- Building, crafting, making, sewing
- Photography, video, editing
- Brainstorming and shitposting and ideating, even if nothing shipped

**The bar:** did you make a thing that wasn't there before? Even a small thing? Then it counts.

---

## 6. Rest

Active recovery. Letting your nervous system come down. This is OUTPUT, not absence of output.

**What counts:**
- Watching a show (deliberately, not doomscrolling-by-default)
- Reading for pleasure
- A nap, lying down, deliberately doing nothing
- A bath, a walk-without-purpose, sitting outside
- Playing a video game, playing with a pet
- Hanging out with friends low-key, no agenda
- Scrolling that you actually chose vs. dissociating into

**What's tricky:** doomscrolling because you can't transition between tasks is closer to executive function tax than rest. Score it lower (5-10) if the user logs it as rest but it sounds avoidant. If they're proud of deliberately resting, score it normally (15-25).
